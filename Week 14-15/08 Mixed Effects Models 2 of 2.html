Random Intercepts Model (Unconditional Means Model)

\[y_{i} = \alpha_{j[i]} + \epsilon_{i}\] \[\alpha_j \sim N(\mu_{\alpha}, \sigma_{\alpha}^2)\] \[\epsilon_i \sim N(0, \sigma_y^2)\]

  • \(y_i\) is the logarithm of the radon measurement in house \(i\).
  • \(\alpha_{j[i]}\) is a random effect of county \(j\). It is the mean of the logarithm of radon measurements in all houses in county \(j\).
  • \(\mu_{\alpha}\) is the mean of the logarithm of radon measurements in all houses across all counties.
  • \(\sigma_{\alpha}^2\) is the variance between counties.
  • \(\sigma_{y}^2\) is the variance within a county.

Varying-Intercept, Fixed-Slope Model

\[y_{i} = \alpha_{j[i]} + \beta (floor)_i + \epsilon_{i}\] \[\alpha_j \sim N(\mu_{\alpha}, \sigma_{\alpha}^2)\] \[\epsilon_i \sim N(0, \sigma_y^2)\]

  • \(y_i\) is the logarithm of the radon measurement in house \(i\).
  • \(\alpha_{j[i]}\) is a random effect of county \(j\). It is the mean of the logarithm of radon measurements taken at the basement in all houses in county \(j\).
  • \(\beta\) is the average difference between the logarithm of radon measurements taken on the first floor and the logarithm of radon measurements taken at the basement.
  • \(\mu_{\alpha}\) is the mean of the logarithm of radon measurements taken at the basement in all houses across all counties.
  • \(\sigma_{\alpha}^2\) is the variance between counties.
  • \(\sigma_{y}^2\) is the variance within a county.

Varying-Intercept, Varying Slope Model

\[y_{i} = \alpha_{j[i]} + \beta_{j[i]} (floor)_i + \epsilon_{i}\] \[\alpha_j \sim N(\mu_{\alpha}, \sigma_{\alpha}^2)\] \[\beta_j \sim N(\mu_{\beta}, \sigma_{\beta}^2)\] \[\epsilon_i \sim N(0, \sigma_y^2)\]

  • \(y_i\) is the logarithm of the radon measurement in house \(i\).
  • \(\alpha_{j[i]}\) is a random effect of county \(j\). It is the mean of the logarithm of radon measurements taken at the basement in all houses in county \(j\).
  • \(\beta_{j[i]}\) is another random effect of county \(j\). It is the average difference between the logarithm of radon measurements taken on the first floor and the logarithm of radon measurements taken at the basement in all houses in county \(j\).
  • \(\mu_{\alpha}\) is the mean of the logarithm of radon measurements taken at the basement in all houses across all counties.
  • \(\mu_{\beta}\) is the mean of the difference between the logarithm of radon measurements taken on the first floor and the logarithm of radon measurements taken at the basement in all houses across all counties.
  • \(\sigma_{\alpha}^2\) and \(\sigma_{\beta}^2\) are variances between counties.
  • \(\sigma_{y}^2\) is the variance within a county.

\[y_{i} = \alpha_{j[i]} + \beta_1 (floor)_i + \beta_2 (uranium)_{j[i]} + \epsilon_{i}\] \[\alpha_j \sim N(\mu_{\alpha}, \sigma_{\alpha}^2)\] \[\epsilon_i \sim N(0, \sigma_y^2)\]

  • \(\alpha_{j[i]}\) is a random effect of county \(j\).
  • \(\beta_1\) is the average difference between the logarithm of radon measurements taken on the first floor and the logarithm of radon measurements taken at the basement controlling for the effect of uranium.
  • \(\beta_2\) is the average change in the logarithm of the radon measurement for each unit change in uranium.
  • \(\mu_{\alpha}\) is the mean of the random intercepts from all the counties.
  • \(\sigma_{\alpha}^2\) is the variance between counties.
  • \(\sigma_{y}^2\) is the variance within a county.
